## 3. Review Strategy

### First 30 days — get to 10 genuine reviews

1. **Free beta access** — offer 3 months free in exchange for an honest review once the customer has actually used the product
2. **Existing customers** — ask for a review once they've completed a real assessment
3. **Personal network** — engineering, compliance, and IT contacts who test the product firsthand

### Guiding reviewers (do NOT send a pre-written review to copy-paste)

G2 and Capterra both prohibit non-genuine reviews — a scripted quote that a reviewer
copies verbatim is not their own words and risks the listing being flagged or removed,
even if the underlying incentive (free access) is disclosed. Instead, give reviewers
real talking points to write their own review from:

- What compliance problem were you trying to solve?
- How long did the assessment actually take, compared to your previous process?
- What surprised you (positive or negative)?
- Would you recommend it, and to whom?

If the review is incentivized (e.g. free months in exchange for a review), disclose
that plainly in the review itself, per G2/Capterra guidelines — most platforms allow
incentivized reviews as long as they're disclosed and written by the actual user.
